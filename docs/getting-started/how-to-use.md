# How to Use This Guide

## Study Approaches

### Progressive Study (Recommended)

Follow the [Study Paths](/study-paths/overview) matched to your rating:

1. Start at your current level.
2. Master each position before moving on.
3. Review periodically to retain knowledge.
4. Advance when you feel comfortable.

### Reference Lookup

Use the material-based sections when you need to:

- Prepare for a specific endgame type
- Analyze a position from your own games
- Look up a position you encountered

### Thematic Study

Explore [Strategic Themes](/strategic-themes/overview) to understand concepts that apply across multiple endgame types.

## Active Learning Tips

### 1. Set Up the Position

Don't just read — use a physical board or software to play through the main line, try alternatives, and practice against an engine.

### 2. Understand, Don't Memorize

Focus on **why** moves work, not just the moves themselves. Understanding the principles lets you handle variations you haven't seen.

### 3. Test Yourself

Cover the solution and try to find the winning method before checking the answer.

### 4. Spaced Repetition

Return to positions after days or weeks. Long-term retention requires periodic review.

### 5. Analyse Your Games

When your games reach an endgame, analyse it thoroughly. Did you know the correct technique? What could you improve?

## Study Schedule Suggestions

| Rating | Time/Week | Focus |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Under 1200 | 1–2 hours | Basic checkmates, K+P vs K |
| 1200–1600 | 2–3 hours | Lucena/Philidor, basic rook endings |
| 1600–2000 | 3–4 hours | Complex pawn endings, minor-piece endings |
| 2000+ | 4+ hours | Exceptions, rare positions, practical play |
