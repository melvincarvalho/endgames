# Recommended Books

## By Skill Level

### Beginner (Under 1200)

| Book | Author | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| Silman's Complete Endgame Course | Jeremy Silman | Rating-based progression — study only what you need for your current level |
| Pandolfini's Endgame Course | Bruce Pandolfini | Clear, concise introductions to essential positions |

### Intermediate (1200-1800)

| Book | Author | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| 100 Endgames You Must Know | Jesus de la Villa | The essential practical positions — every club player should know these |
| Endgame Strategy | Mikhail Shereshevsky | Theme-based approach teaching strategic thinking in endgames |
| A Guide to Chess Endings | Max Euwe & David Hooper | Classic text covering fundamental endgame principles |

### Advanced (1800+)

| Book | Author | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| Dvoretsky's Endgame Manual | Mark Dvoretsky | The gold standard — comprehensive and rigorous, for serious players |
| Fundamental Chess Endings | Müller & Lamprecht | Single-volume encyclopedia — excellent reference for looking up any ending |

### Specialized

| Book | Author | Topic |
|------|--------|-------|
| Rook Endings | Levenfish & Smyslov | Definitive rook endgame reference — a classic |
| Secrets of Pawn Endings | Müller & Lamprecht | Deep pawn endgame coverage with exercises |
| Bishop Endings | Jon Speelman | Detailed treatment of bishop endgame types |
| Practical Rook Endings | Victor Korchnoi | Insights from one of the greatest endgame players |

## How to Choose

1. **Start with Silman** if you're under 1600
2. **Add de la Villa** when you reach 1600
3. **Use Dvoretsky** as a reference when you reach 2000
4. **Specialize** with topical books as needed
